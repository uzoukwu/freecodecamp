Vera Wang

The most influential bridal designer

Vera Ellen Wang was born on June 27, 1949, in New York City, New York. The daughter of Chinese immigrants.She attended the elite Chapin School and the School of American Ballet, before enrolling at Sarah Lawrence College. During her sophomore year, Wang briefly studied abroad at the Sorbonne in Paris but returned to the United States to complete her degree in art history.

She has been skating from the age of 8 until now at the age of 71. Wang competed professionally as a figure skater in her teens. She and her partner, James Stuart, placed fifth in the junior pairs competition at the 1968 U.S. National Champions

Wang received diffenrent awards over her career, including the 1994 Girl Scout Council’s Woman of Distinction Award, CFDA's womenswear designer of the year, 2005 and André Leon Talley Lifetime Achievement Award.

Wang may be known for evening and bridal, but she has a T-shirt obsession. She has an entire room filled with nothing other than T-shirts. “The tops are all carefully organized by designer and are almost exclusively in what Wang terms 'Armani' colors: gray, black, navy.”

“It doesn’t matter what it is: being a mom, teaching, whatever you love. If you love it, it’ll get you through the hard times, and if you don’t, it’s not authentic or real anyway. When you love something, every day goes by in 10 minutes."
